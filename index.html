🟢 Basics

Everything a first-time player needs to step onto the court with confidence.

The rules in 60 seconds

  • Two teams of 6 players each, separated by a net.
  • Each team gets a maximum of 3 touches to return the ball.
  • You can't hit the ball twice in a row yourself.
  • A point is scored when the ball lands in the opponent's court, goes out off them, or they commit a fault.
  • Matches are played to 25 points (win by 2), best of 3 or 5 sets.

Court & positions

  • Front row (3 players): attack and block near the net.
  • Back row (3 players): defend, dig, and pass.
  • The setter runs the offense — think of them as the quarterback.
  • The libero is a defensive specialist who wears a different color.

Core skill: The underhand serve

  1. Stand with your non-dominant foot forward.
  2. Hold the ball in your non-hitting hand at waist height.
  3. Swing your hitting arm like a pendulum, striking with the heel of your palm.
  4. Follow through toward your target.

Core skill: The forearm pass (bump)

  1. Make a platform: stack one hand in the other, thumbs together and level.
  2. Bend your knees, stay low, and keep arms straight.
  3. Let the ball hit your forearms — don't swing; use your legs to lift.
  4. Angle your platform toward the target.

🏋️ Beginner drills

  • Wall passing: bump the ball against a wall 20 times without losing control.
  • Partner serving: serve back and forth from 15 feet, then step back as you improve.
  • Ball-handling: toss and catch above your forehead in the "setting" triangle to learn hand position.

🟡 Medium

Sharpen your technique and start thinking like a team.

Rotations & positioning

  • Players rotate clockwise one spot each time your team wins the serve back.
  • Learn the 6 rotational positions (1 = server, then 6, 5, 4, 3, 2).
  • Avoid overlap faults — keep your relative order until the ball is served.
  • Understand switching: after the serve you can move to your specialist position.

Skill: The set

  1. Form a triangle with your thumbs and index fingers above your forehead.
  2. Contact the ball with your finger pads — not your palms.
  3. Push through the ball using wrists and legs together.
  4. Square your hips and shoulders to your target hitter.

Skill: The spike (attack)

  1. Use a 3-step or 4-step approach (left-right-left for righties).
  2. Swing both arms back, then up to load your jump.
  3. Snap your wrist over the top of the ball for topspin.
  4. Contact the ball at the highest point of your reach.

Skill: Blocking

  • Start with hands up near shoulder height, watching the setter.
  • Jump after the hitter and press your hands over the net.
  • "Seal" the net — reduce the gap so the ball can't slip through.
  • Angle your hands to deflect the ball back into their court.

Skill: The overhand serve

  1. Toss the ball 2–3 feet above your hitting shoulder.
  2. Draw your elbow back like an archer.
  3. Contact the center of the ball with a firm, flat hand.
  4. Follow through toward your target zone.

🏋️ Intermediate drills

  • Set & hit: partner tosses, you set, they spike — then switch roles.
  • Serve-to-zones: aim serves at each of the 6 court zones; score points for accuracy.
  • Pepper: pass, set, hit continuously with a partner to build all-round control.

🔴 Hard

Advanced tactics, systems, and high-pressure skills used by competitive players.

Offensive systems

  • 5-1: one setter runs the whole game — most common at high levels.
  • 6-2: two setters (setting from the back row) for six front-row attackers.
  • Run tempo offense: quick sets (1s), shoots, and back-row attacks to beat the block.
  • Use combination plays (e.g. "X" or slide) to isolate hitters against one blocker.

Defensive systems

  • Perimeter defense: defenders spread to the court edges to cover deep tips and cross shots.
  • Rotational (rotate) defense: players shift based on the set and blocker positioning.
  • Read the hitter's shoulder and approach angle to anticipate the shot.
  • Coordinate block + dig: diggers position around the block's "shadow".

Skill: The jump serve

  1. Toss the ball high and in front, into the court.
  2. Use a spike approach and attack the serve at full extension.
  3. Snap for topspin (topspin jump serve) or hit flat for a float.
  4. Target seams between passers and short zones to disrupt reception.

Skill: Advanced attacking

  • Tooling the block: aim off the blocker's hands to score off deflections.
  • Tips & roll shots: soft placement to open court when the block is set.
  • Line vs. angle: read the block and hit the undefended lane.
  • Back-row attacks: hit from behind the 3-meter (10-ft) line.

Reading the game

  • Watch the opposing setter's hands and body to predict the set.
  • Track hitters' tendencies — where do they hit under pressure?
  • Serve strategically to force weak passers out of system.
  • Communicate constantly: call the ball, the block, and the coverage.

🏋️ Advanced drills

  • Wash drills: chain serve → pass → attack sequences that must be won to score.
  • Blocking read reps: coach sets randomly; blockers must react and seal in time.
  • Pressure serving: must make 8 of 10 jump serves in target zones or start over.
  • 6-on-6 scrimmage with specific tactical constraints (e.g. quick-set only).
